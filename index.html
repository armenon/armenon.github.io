Baby Location
Complete Medical Solutions

  • Client
    • District Health Board, Manukau County, Auckland, New Zealand
  • References
    • Apple store
    • Web link
  • Role
    • UI/UX Designer
    • Visual Designer
    • Mobile Application Developer
  • Time
    • 1.5 years (including pilot study)
  • Abstract
    • Baby Location is an iOS application that interfaces with an Internet of Things (IoT) based tracking platform to track the location of newborn infants in the hospitals coming under the jurisdiction of the District Health Board of Manukau County in Auckland, New Zealand.
    • My Role
      • I was involved in both designing and developing the interface for the application and the overall user experience including how users interacted with various technical artefacts in the system.
    • Why was this needed?
      • The District Health Board of Manukau County wanted to increase the security at the hospitals under their jurisdiction. They wanted to harness the power of the Internet of Things to see if they could install a future proof system that could ultimately be used to improve their overall security system. To this end, they called out to many companies to test their solutions as a proof of concept at one of the biggest hospitals under their care - Middlemore Hospital.
    • The Pilot Study
      • Complete Medical Solutions and WiSilica undertook a month long pilot study at Middlemore Hospital, Auckland in order to test and prove the effectiveness of their solution. The pilot study involved using a prototype application that I designed.
      • The prototype application evolved into one of the first versions of the tracking applications used by WiSilica.
    • The Issues
      • The primary users of this system were the nurses at the respective hospitals, the system was planned to be installed. However, due to their busy schedules the overhead introduced by the new system would have been an additional burden for them.The first version had flows that were too long winded and complicated for them to use. So the system had to be redesigned to be quick and simple to use.
      • The administrators of the system also needed an interface to install, configure and monitor the system, which required a more technical interface. Thus the interface had a dichotomy between having to be simple and having to be technical
      • One of the main complaints that the nurses had during the pilot study was that system was too technically challenging to use. The nurses were people who were just about comfortable with smartphones but not more. The flow had to be redesigned so that the system did most of the heavy lifting and was also ‘technically simple’ to use
    • Solution Goals
      • Simplify the application’s functionality to show primary goals in the first UI
      • Perform complex actions with simple steps
      • Use a simple design language
      • Use illustrations to indicate actions
      • Role based UIs
    • Prep Screen
      • Login
      • Prep Screen
      • Add a baby tag
      • Track a Baby
      • Alerts
      • Technical Screens
        • Add Devices
        • Diagnostics
        • Users
    • Evaluations and Feedback
      • Owing to the difficulties in logistics, proper evaluations and usability tests could not be conducted. The majority of the testing done was functional and done remotely, in India.
      • The nurses responded very positively to the changes in UX and the design language. They felt that the new application was easy to use and visually appealing.
      • The management at the District Health Board were also pleased with the functioning of the system and application and were considering utilising the system to track all the assets in the hospital
